Exotic dance moves

Yes. you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question,
my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Plynesian chicken dance

A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC. a whole village would dance around
in a cicle like chikens, to encourage their livestock or be "fruitfil".

Icelandic bronian shuffle

Before the Icelander developed fire as a means of getting warms, they used to practice this dance, which
involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and a shuffling their bodies around in imperceptably
very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired
modem styles such as Tweking.

Artic robot dance

An interesting example of historic misinformation, english explorers in the 1960s belivied to have
discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stiled movements, being practiced by inhabitants
of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this
because they were really cold.
